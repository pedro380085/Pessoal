Up until I got in college, I used to be severely bullied. It used to be more intense on elementary school, but it kept misering constant during high school. One of the things I kept being picked out about was my inability to talk or relate to women; indeed, it was something I had to skill at all.

So when I got in college, I set a guideline of actions I would work on to solve my shyness when talking to people. Professionally, I improved very much, directly behaved by a sense of overall cohesion with where my company was going.

But personally, I was depressed. I got to be a more talkative and outgoing person, but I could not approach women on parties without being severely out of my comfort zone. I learnt everything I could, from talking to my friends, watching videos, reading books and partying four times a week.

I truly was a party guy. I posed myself as one. But deep inside, I still suffered very much from the fact that every single approach was a crush of emotions, from  anxiety to denial, everything propelled by adrenaline. I couldn't apply the same know-how I had from my professional interactions to my personal life without being crushed by a sentiment of overall depression.

But what should I do? One of the things I've learnt as my business got more mature was that context is one of the greatest and most important things an institution can have. Nothing is an absolute truth, everythings depends upon the context it is inserted upon.

So it got me thinking, if I cannot prohibit myself from partying as an way to avoid depression, what should I do? One of the things I like the most about parties is the music itself; being able to dance and do whatever I feel about doing when listening to something is beyond describable. Also, talking to people that I had no previous contact with and really getting to know them is very exciting and fun.

In simple words, my problems with parties is that I used to see them as work. I had set myself to improve dearly throughout college, so everything for me was just about proving that I could do it, no matter what it was. I was supposed to be Superman, and no questions should ever been asked.

But then I applied the second lesson learnt from my company: everything must have a meaning you feel part of. Doing something because you just need to improve is a very weak argument; you must find something else, something stronger, to really devote yourself to.

Maturity gave me the ability to deny things that in the past have caused me greatest pain but I wasn't able to work on their obvious solutions; it means that, even if I had set myself to be a party womanizer, now I can remove it from my life and still enjoy parties very much.

So, no more dating (or whatever you got to call it) on parties. No kissing and anything beyond it. Parties are supposed to be a moment of leisure for myself, so I'm supposed to do the things that I've identified myself with, such as dancing and getting to know people. No need to be a virile player where I don't need to.

Everything is about finding people that can I can have a deep connection with, someone I can really talk to without playing the script delineated by pickup attempts. Fewer people, higher intrinsic value.

Remove what causes you deep pain and will be able to live much better.

PS 1: Ow, and your Facebook feed: don't care about your friends posting photos about their newly found girlfriends while partying; if you are a content based man, meaningless relationships just tend to increase your moment-based depressions over time.

PS 2: I know that for some people this text is the exact definition of how much social ineptitude a person can have; but that's exactly the point of it, to show that a person that doesn't really conform to what society defined as ideal can overcome it as a way to find real, crude solutions to its personal problems.
