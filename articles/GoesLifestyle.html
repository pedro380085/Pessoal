Our path

Life is meant to be super simple. Sometimes we end up creating complex situations to justify. As a young CEO of a tech startup, it's very common for me to extend multiple working days till infinity, and is very common for me to go to bed til very late in the evening.

Being usually on a very tight schedule, I've developed three simple rules that are essentially enough. I've been doing them since I'm sixteen and, even though I live in a city 1500 kilometers away from my closest parent, I've never been sick longer than two days (damn flu!).

The rules

These rules develop no scientific value other than myself as a test case. I've developed them based on multiple experiences, which include multiple books reading, biographies of great and highly achieving people, biological analysis of the general human being and thousands of days of practice.

Here they go:

  • Sleep
  • Eat
  • Exercise

        

Sleep, Eat, Exercise, or SEE, for short, is a very simple acronym for a very simple lifestyle.

Steve Jobs once said: "Genius is not the man who can make complex things, genius is the man who can make complex things look simple". That's exactly what SEE is about, it's a simple way of keeping the essential things of life in focus while your life can be enjoyed on its completeness.

My mom kept telling me when I was growing up: "Exaggeration is the greatest evil of all. Equilibrium is the only thing that keeps you in control of your life quality. Even water, which is considered something good, can cause death if drank in high quantities".

For many years I've followed a method similar to the ones found in many military facilities, where sleeping and eating should be done whenever possible. This is something great to do if you fighting a war, but in our case, that ends up being more of a liability than a real benefit to our lifestyle. So try to keep with your balance and equilibrium.

Sleep

Sleeping is not about resting your head in some random pile of manure on a closed barn after spending man hours working.

You have to sleep six hours a day. That's a rule, not an advice. If you sleep twelve hours in a day and then stay woken up for the next forty-eight hours, that's up to you, but I can guarantee that SEE you probably fail on you.

There is something called sleep accumulation. According to my uncle, who is a very good doctor, it doesn't really work, but for me it has enabled some long periods of partying when I've prepared myself for it.

It basically is, you sleep more a single day to compensate for you lack of sleep on the other day, not going below your daily sleeping hours for less than two hours.

When you accumulate sleeping hours, your deposit loses about 40% of its effectiviness. It means, that even if you sleep twelve hours on a single day, your deposit will only have about 60% of your sparing hours, which will amount to 3.6h or 216 minutes (12h - 6h => 6h * 0.6 => 3.6h).

Eat

Eat food! Real food, not pizza with a bunch of soda for lunch. It's simple: you have to eat three times a day. Pay attention that I've never said that those must be breakfast, lunch and dinner. We are tireless workers, and sometimes we sleep until a late time where breakfast is no longer an option. It's not because of such a small detail that we'll break our simple set of rules.

Always give an interval between our meals. If you slept until late, don't eat like a pig at midday followed by a light dinner. Try to separate your meals into small quantities, such that you can complete your day at a late time with a portion of delicious fruits.

Exercise

That's the part where SEE usually falls apart. You see, people cannot concentrate on handling a set of exercises of their choices for a very lengthy period of time. Even though sometimes they start well, time usually erodes their self esteem down, and that's the moment when results stop to keep coming.

In India, it's very common for people to buy small quantities of soap and shampoo. Even though their limiting factor is money, the same logic can be applied in here, where you constraint is time.

How many people do you know that work out at Christmas? Or New Year's Eve? I bet that you know some gym freaks, but here we are talking about casual people with busy schedules and infrequent visits to their families.

Myself, I've kept a selected pair of exercises: every single day of my life since I turned 16 I make, at least, 30 push ups and 30 ab exercises. In recent months I've raised these numbers to 70 push ups and 60 ab exercises, but that's because I've had some practice over time.

What is amazing about these is that they take less than 8 minutes to be completed! That's less time that it takes to eat a sack of popcorn from the microwave (and man, those are good!).

When I'm exercising, I always have some music on. That helps me concentrate on the very intensive task ahead, so I can accomplish everything in less than 8 minutes.
